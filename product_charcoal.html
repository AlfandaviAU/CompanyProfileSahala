Coconut Shell Charcoal

Coconut shell charcoal is an important product derived from coconut shells. Shell charcoal is widely used as a household and industrial fuel. Shell charcoal is also used in the production of activated carbon. Activated carbon made from coconut shells has certain advantages because the raw material can absorb certain types of molecules. The shell is carbonized using methods such as the pit method, drum method, and destructive distillation.
Cocoshell charcoal is very suitable as a raw material for activated carbon and briquettes. Otherwise, you can immediately use the for cooking, grilling and grilling to enjoy green energy. Baking in coconut charcoal means getting an eco-friendly and sustainable product. Also, the fire does not add chemicals to the coconut charcoal, so it burns cleanly. It's a real wooden grill rather than a charcoal briquette. There is no coconut flavor. The smell of charcoal is fairly neutral, except for the smell of grill smoke, which focuses on grilled food
Coconut Shell Charcoal has proven to be a suitable material for the development of golf courses At golf courses, before planting grass, a layer of charcoal from the coconut shell is laid down with sand and other biomass to the specified depth.
